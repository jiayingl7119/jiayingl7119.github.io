Web Page Project

Context

After learning about IDE, I was assigned a partner project to make a adventure type game. During this project, there was a lot of creativity put into it. There were also many merging and communication during the project with my partner.

Content

As stated beforehand, we used IDE. Specifically cs50.dev. For the entire time doing this project, there had to be headings and paragraphs. Most of all were the links to the different options of the adventure game. This is the general layout of the webpage. This was all included in the planning page. We also added more ideas in that planning when we did have new ideas. Which in this case was not used due to not having enough time.

Challenges

One challenge I encountered during this project is the linking between the files. Sometimes the links wouldn't work. Like linking the ending back to the README.md. However, this was fixed after reviewing other pieces of work.

Another challenge I encountered was teamwork. After all, if your partner was not in class, you would have no idea what they are doing or if they were doing the project at all.

Takeaways

One of things I am taught by this project is to trust the process. The reason why is because for one, the first things that were added did not look well. But working through it, it started looking better and better. Until it finally reaches to the point of it looking better than what was started with.

Another lesson I learned is to trust your teammate and also give them advice. that way, the project will be easier for you and the person you're working with. Plus, you can actually get some great insight from the other person if you don't know what to do.

Next Steps

I plan on working on more projects more by myself than with others. The reason being that I can't really trust anyone because they end up doing things last minute. All in all, this was a fun experience but a stressful one. I probably will not be doing this again unless needed to or asked to. Again, still the same goals because I believe that projects with others is more stressful than doing it by yourself.
